Balancing Online Savings and Stablecoin Interest Rates

We’ve all felt it. The financial uncertainty of the post COVID-19 world. Some consumers are taking advantage of some of the effects and getting good deals on car loans and mortgages. To the saver however, these dropping interest rates are affecting our passive income.

As banks offer lower interest rates to consumers they have to in-turn offer lower rates to savings accounts. I’ve been watching my high interest savings account’s rate, along with my passive income, drop steadily through 2019 and sharply after the coronavirus outbreak.

High interest savings accounts are one of the safest, federally insured places to keep your money. Most high interest savings accounts are offered by online banks. Online banking is able to offer higher interest rates by maintaining lower overhead than traditional brick and mortar banks. These savings allow them offer more competitive rates for savers.

Stablecoins are a low risk crypto currency that maintain a 1 to 1 value with the USD. Institutions utilizing stablecoins are able to offer much higher interest rates because of the dramatically decreased overhead achieved by using blockchain technology. Many of these crypto banks use over-collateralized loans to generate low risk interest from their holdings. These crypto bank accounts can offer up to 10x the rate that an online savings account can.

Remember that crytpo currency is an asset, not a fiat currency. Your assets will need to be liquidated in order to be used as USD. A traditional investment strategy for investment assets such as precious metals is to be no more than 10% of your overall portfolio. Lets split that 10% between precious metals and crypto currency. 5% of your savings converted into stablecoin and earning 10x interest is a noticeable offset.

If you have $3,000 in a savings account making 0.8% interest, you are making $2.00 a month. If you take 5% of your savings, $150, and put it into a stablecoin savings account at 8% interest (yes 8% is real), you are making $1.00 a month. The remaining $2,850 is still making $1.90/m in your online savings account. Your total is now $2.90 a month, bumping your over-all interest rate to about 1.15%.

How safe is your stablecoin? Many crypto bank accounts have independent insurance. Some stablecoins are even offered by centralized institutions with a guarantee to maintain 1 to 1 value with USD. Moving your money from a high interest savings account to your checking account will take anywhere between to 1 day and 1 week if the transfer is between banks. Transferring stablecoins between wallets takes well under an hour and converting to USD through an exchange is often available next day. Taking away the volatility of traditional cryptocurrency, stablecoin’s are quite safe.

Taking advantage of stablecoin savings accounts can help save your passive income from dropping savings rates. By balancing the risk and opportunity of high interest savings accounts and stablecoin accounts you can help to protect yourself from falling interest rates.
