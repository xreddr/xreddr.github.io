Sustainable Financial Planning

Whale sharks sustain themselves by means of filter feedings, the process of filtering water in order to capture many small food particles suspended in their environment as they swim. Conceptualy using small capital gains (food) to fuel carefully managed expenses (swimming) to create a financial entity that sustains itself without the expense of capital. This foundation for monetizable properties gives new investors a low risk opertunity to create a passive income.

What is Whale Shark

Whale Shark combines passive income and careful budgeting in order to provide start-up funding for websites. The basic idea is to purchase a domain name within the limits of earnings from an interest-bearing account. Think about it as having a store-front that pays its own rent without selling anything.

People now more than ever are in need of alternative incomes and financial safety nets. Whale Shark's asset allocation strategy gives passive income with the potential for growth that also acts as a savings account. You do not need to spend money to make money. You simply need to have money to make money.

Of course, there is a lot of hard work that accompanies such a small budget. The kind of hard work that forces you to build things from the ground up and learn the skills you need to be successful. The time, however, is irrelevant as the project is sustainably paying for itself. What you can do with something when you have forever to do it is up to you.
